What we do

Objectives of the project

For an equal opportunity in our society today, ensuring quality education among girls is a prerequisite towards the realization of that goal. However, this is hardly the case in most African Countries, especially in poor urban and slum areas where disadvantaged girls lack sanitary towels primarily due to the high costs.


The Heart Project, a new project that is to run for ten years, seeks to address this challenge by enhancing the accessibility to sanitary towels through the provision of free sanitary pads to the disadvantaged girls in Taita, Loitoktok, Ukunda, Msambweni, Kinango, Kibera Slums, and Liboi.


The Main Objectives of the project are the following:

  1. To enhance the retention of disadvantaged girls in primary and secondary schools
    through the provision of free sanitary towels.
  3. To empower women and girls in rural and marginalised communities by providing psychosocial support and welfare education.

Project Impacts

Community Impacts of the Heart Project

Quality Education by Ensuring the retention of girls in schools.

Improved Health and Well-being.

Happy lives through recreation and co-curriculum activities.

Improved lives of vulnurable women i.e. victims of injustices.
